A Hidden Source of Energy Waste

One of the most common forms of energy waste happens quietly, every day.

During cold seasons, many households heat indoor spaces while windows are partially or fully open. What feels like a small, temporary action results in continuous heat loss and unnecessary energy consumption. Warm air escapes immediately, forcing heaters to run longer to maintain comfort. Over time, this small habit compounds into a steady drain on energy resources and household budgets.

Because the waste is gradual and invisible, it often goes unnoticed, accumulating into higher energy demand, increased costs, and avoidable environmental impact. The effect multiplies across neighborhoods and cities, contributing to peak-load stress on power grids and higher emissions during the coldest months.

Awareness as the First Step

Rather than automating behaviour blindly, our approach focuses on making environmental conditions visible and understandable.

By revealing when heating and ventilation work against each other, users are supported in making informed decisions that reduce waste without sacrificing comfort.

The goal is not to control homes, but to enable more responsible everyday choices.
